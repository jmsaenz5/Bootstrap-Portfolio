About Me

Jason Saenz was born and raised in Houston, Texas, where he did some things and had some experiences. After he graduated from high school, he wandered around Texas, attending various universities, and working during the summers as a river and hiking guide in Alaska. He ultimately graduated from Houston Baptist University with a double major in professional writing and mass communication.

Even before graduation, Jason began a position as a copy editor for the Times-News in Twin Falls, Idaho. After moving back to his hometown, he worked as a copywriter for various oil and gas companies’ marketing departments. Unsatisfied with his job, Jason decided to move full-time to Juneau, Alaska, where he worked for the Boy Scouts of America as the district executive of Southeast Alaska. He explored the beautiful islands of the region while running the area’s day-to-day operations.

Despite his many adventures in the Last Frontier, Jason missed the mean, humid streets of Houston. He returned to Texas and spent the next two years teaching high school English. Still unsatisfied, Jason discovered a love for coding. And so here we are.
